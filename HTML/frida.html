Frida Kahlo

The Great Mexican Artist

Frida Kahlo's Timeline:

  • 1907-Born in Coyoacán, Mexico, La Casa Azul, The Blue House
  • 1913-Contracts polio and her right leg & foot are left deformed
  • 1922-Becomes a member of "Las Cachuchas" a political group that supported nationalist-socialist ideas
  • 1924-Frida helps her father with his photography business which cultivates her artistic skills
  • 1925-El Accidente, a tram crashes into the bus Frida is riding on, she barely survives and is left with a broken pelvis & spine in addition to other severe injuries. She intimiately paints the physical and emotional pain from this incident in her future work
  • 1926-She recovers from the accident and paints her first self-portrait "Self-Portrait in a Velvet Dress"
  • 1928-Frida meets Diego Rivera, an artist and he encourages her to continue to paint. She begins a romantic relationship with him
  • 1929-Frida Kahlo and Diego Rivera marry despite him being twice her age. She paints her second self-portrait, "Time Flies"
  • 1930-Due to the accident, Frida has trouble bearing children and undergoes an abortion. Frida and Diego go to San Francisco where Diego has been commissioned to paint murals. Frida paints "Frieda and Diego Rivera"
  • 1931-Frida's leg worsens and she is hospitalized. She meets Dr. Leo Eloesser, a bone surgeon and Diego's friend who is her go-to doctor for the rest of her life. One of Frida Kahlo's painting is publicly shown for the first time at the"Sixth Annual Exhibition of the San Francisco Society of Women Artists"
  • 1932-Frida and Diego move to Detroit as Diego has been commissioned to paint yet another mural for the Ford Motor Company. Frida has a miscarriage and stays at Henry Ford Hospital where she goes on to paint "Henry Ford Hospital" depicting the miscarriage
  • 1934-Back in Mexico, Frida has health and marital troubles. She has another abortion and Diego has an affair with her younger sister. The couple separates
  • 1935-Diego and Frida reconcile but agree to live separate lives
  • 1937-Leon Trotsky, a Marxist revolutionary and his wife are granted asylum in Mexico. Frida offers her "Blue House" to them and Frida has an affair with Leon. Frida's paintings are shown publicly for the first time in Mexico
  • 1938-Frida travels to New York for an exhibition of 25 of her paintings and she sells half of them. French poet and surrealist Andre Breton calls Frida a "Surrealist Artist"
  • 1939-Kahlo travels to Paris for an exhibition of her work and the Louvre purchases one of her works-the first 20th century painting by a Mexican artist. Diego and Frida divorce
  • 1940-Two more of Frida's works are exhibited "The Two Fridas" and "The Wounded Table" at the Gallery of Mexican Art. Trotsky is assassinated. "The Two Fridas" is shown at the Museum of Modern Art. Diego and Frida reconcile and remarry
  • 1941-Frida's father dies which leaves her depressed and in poor health
  • 1943-More of Frida's work is exhibited at galleries throughout the United States. She becomes an instructor at "La Esmeralda" & holds classes in her Coyoacán home with 4 students who become "Los Fridos"
  • 1944-Frida's physical health declines. Frida continues to exhibit her work and she starts a diary
  • 1947-Frida celebrates her 40th birthday and her painting "Self-portrait as a Tehuana" is exhibited
  • 1949-Frida paints "Diego and I" and "The Love Embrace of the Universe, the Earth (Mexico), Me, Diego, and Mr. Xolotl"
  • 1950-Frida is hospitalized and has 7 operations on her spine. She is mostly in the hospital and occasionally paints when she feels well enough
  • 1952-Frida switches to painting still life paintings. It is theorized she started to paint still life because of the painkillers and alcohol she was consuming that affected her detail and precision
  • 1953-Frida has her first solo exhibition but the doctor says she is not well enough to attend. Her determination and perserverance are unstoppable. She decides to transport her bed to the gallery where she lays while greeting her friends who have come to see her work
  • 1954-Frida dies in the Blue House & suicide is suspected but never confirmed. Her body is cremated and placed in a pre-Colombian urn on display in the Blue House. 600 people come to pay their respects at her funeral
